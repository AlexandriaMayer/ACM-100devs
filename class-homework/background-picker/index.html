Metro Line 1

"Mainstreet"

The first line in the system, openned in 1979 as a 4.9 mile (7.9km), 8 station loop around Downtown Austin and the University of Texas. This configuration remained until 1992, when the line was extended 3.3 miles (5.3km) down South Congress to the St. Elmo Center. The line has been expanded multiple times since, now reaching 19 miles (30km) from Southpark to Tech Ridge via South Congress, Guadalupe, and Lamar. WTXMA estimates Metro Line 1 is the busiest in the system, at 370,000 average weekday ridership; Trains reach crush loads during rush hour. Capacity and frequency will be tripled once the Metro Line 2 ReBore reaches estimated completion in 2014.

Facts at a Glance:
First Openned: 1979
Length: 19 miles (30km)
Number of Stations: 26
Average Daily Rivership: 370,000
Future Extensions: Onion Creek & IH-35, Summer 2013.

Metro Line 2

"Westside"

The second line in the system, Metro Line 2 opened in 1995 with a 3 mile (4.8 km) extension from the downtown loop, running along West 5th Street, under Town Lake and Zilker Park, through the Zilker neighborhood, and to Lamar & Oltorf Boulevards. The line took over the eastern portion of the downtown loop, and further extensions in 2003 via 38th St brought access to the Mabry Portal Complex. The line currently runs 26 miles (41.9 km) from Slaughter Lane & Menchaca Boulevard to the North Intermodal Center via the old Missouri Pacific mainline, Zilker Park, 5th Street, Red River, Guadalupe, 38th, and Burnet Road, with a 1 mile (1.7km) branch via 38th Street to the aforementioned Mabry Portal.

Facts at a Glance:
First Openned: 1995
Length: 15 miles (24.1 kilometers) A branch, 26 miles (41.8km) B branch
Number of Stations: 35
Average Daily Rivership: 340,000
Future Extensions: RM 620 in Fall 2013, Central Lamar re-route in Summer 2014.
