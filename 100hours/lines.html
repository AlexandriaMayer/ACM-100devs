Metro Lines

Metro Line 1

"Mainstreet"

Southpark to Tech Ridge via Congress, Guadalupe, Lamar

The first line in the system, M1 connects the most massive portions of Austin and the busiest travel cooridor.

Technical Specifications: Standard Guage. 750v DC Third Rail. 3m wide cars

Metro Line 2

"University"

Menchaca/Slaughter to Brushy Creek via the UPRR, Zilker, Downtown, Burnet, and Mopac

The M2 covers West-Central Austin and the eastern side of Downtown Austin. The southside alignment is a symbol of both the excess and prudence of late 90s transit planning in Austin, with a deep bore tunnel under Zilker Park near Barton Springs, leading to a surface alignment along the UPPR. The northside aligment changed multiple times, with the subway and elevated along Burnet Road being the most consistent feature.

Technical Specifications: Standard Guage. 750v DC Third Rail. 3m wide cars

Metro Line 3

"Liberty"

Manor to Bee Caves via the A&H East, 5th Street, Redbud, Horizon, and the Lake Country Interurban

The M3 started as metro line upgrading the old commuter services along the A&H east. The M3 was the first line to affected by the "de-looping" plans around the turn of the millenium, as the downtown aligment changed from a tangent along the southern side of the downtown loop to a new east-west downtown subway. The M3 also was the first metro line planned ahead as part of a private development, when the Redbud Corporation pre-bored tunnels during the refurbishment of the Redbud Quary & establishment of the westside business district. The M3 represents a symbol of the city's attempts to bridge the divide between the richer, whiter West Austin and pooer, minority-majority East Austin.

Technical Specifications: Standard Guage. 750v DC Third Rail. 3m wide cars

Metro Line 4

"Research"

WBIGA to Lakeline via South Intermodal, the D&P, the A&H north, and Research Boulevard

The M4 is the local service running along the D&P East and North-Central subdivisions, running from Southeast to Northwest Austin.

Technical Specifications: Standard Guage. 25kV 60 Hz AC Overhead Caternary. 3m wide cars

Metro Line 5

"Longhorn"

Oak Hill to Parmer Ln via Ben White, Lamar, Manor Rd, Mueller, Cameron Road

The M5 started life as a proposed branch from the downtown loop to Robert Mueller airport and the planned Manor airport. Changes in the city plans during the 1990s meant the M5 would be connecting to a major redevlopment instead of an airport. The southwest portion of the M5 was originally proposed as a branch of the M2, but was made into a seperate line during the de-looping phase of the 90s and 00s.

Metro Line 6

"Justice"

Hornsby Bend to Genoa via MLK Jr Boulevard, Rosewood, 12th St, Enfield Rd, Redbud, High Road

The M6 was pressed for by East Austin community leaders to fill in gaps from the M3 and improve East-West connectivity. With the construction of the line, travel times for over a hundred thousand transit-dependent people were cut in half, and a quarter million affordable homes were constructed between Hornsby Bend and Exposition Boulevard. Further extensions to the Redbud Center and Genoa dramatically increased accessibility to high-paying jobs in West Austin.

Metro Line 7

"Crosstown"

Line 7-A: Decker Lake to Redbud via Colony Park, Manor Rd, Mueller, 38th St, Westlake Drive

Line 7-B: Harris Branch to Genoa via Manor Rd, Mueller, 38th St, Westlake Drive

The M7 was a product of de-interlining the M2's branch to the Marby Portal. Initially starting as a shuttle service, extensions of the M7's subway have suplanted the T1's east-west crosstown connections, improving travel times and increasing capacity between the largest destinations outside of Downtown Austin.
