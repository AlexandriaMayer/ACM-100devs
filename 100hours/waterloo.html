When I talk about my urban fantast version of Austin, people usually ask me, "Allie, why is the city like this?" and the usual response is "It came to me in a dream"
No, literally. I have vivid dreams and I remember a lot of them. In late 2021, I drew out a map of Austin according to what's seen in my dreams.
This map became basis for the world building I was doing for a urban fantasy story.
I still need to write the story.

Let's start with

The Loop

In 2017, I had a dream that Austin had a subway loop running downtown instead of Capital Metro's existing commuter rain.
It was 5 miles long, running under Guadalupe Street, Dean Keaton, Red River Rd, and Cesar Chavez St. It had stations at Republic Square, South Congress, the Convention Center, E 12th, E MLK Jr, Dean Keaton/San Jacinto, W 24th, and W 12th
There was one question I asked before I woke up: "Is it better for me to ride a subway that doesn't go beyond downtown, or a infrequent commuter train that sort of goes to my part of town?"

The Answer Is: God, yes.

Do you know how many billions in taxpayer dollars are saved because someone built a subway tunnel in the 1980s? Even if the rest of the system is at grade, a downtown subway loop puts Austin lightyears ahead of what we are currently fightign to build today. 5 miles is thrice as large as The famous loop of the Chicago L.

Repeating patterns

In my fictional version of Austin, the main metro lines tend to follow the real world alighnments of Austin's proposed and planned rapid transit routes. This comes from Federal Transit Adminstration guidelines; grants are given to projects that have the most promising ridership and the most potential to serve communities in need. The facts and data are in agreement, and it's a reasonable starting point for my own worldbuilding.
